This project combines people from many different labs, working on a range of different species , to see if and how the length of acclimation affects the behaviour of individuals.

Why? For most behavioural and cognitive tests, individuals need to be habituated to the testing conditions before they start behaving normally. What is the optimal duration of acclimation trials, how long should you expect to wait before your chosen model species engages in assays?

There is lots of institutional knowledge, but not much published data to guide researchers in general or for individual species.

To start with, we are testing a range of common – and less common – model species in simple open-field behavioural assays across two different lengths of acclimation trials (10 and 30 mins), with individuals tested over three successive days.
